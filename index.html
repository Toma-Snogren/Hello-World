COVID Unit Experience

Working as a CNA during COVID on an ICU COVID Floor

Boston Medical Center

Boston Medical Center was one of the hospitals in the Boston area that was overwhelmed in the summer due to an influx of COVID patients. Many step down units, ICU, and pediatric were transformed into COVID units. Many medical staff became sick with COVID, had to move from their families to protect them, and worked extra shifts. Personel were exposed to high stress environments, with little protective equipment at the start of the pandemic.

Here is a link to #BMCproud youtube video. It shows the resilience of the Boston Medical Team, and the strength of the community

I have included some bullet points on key facts about the COVID unit and healthcare workers

  • PPE consisted of;scrubs, N-95, 2 pairs of gloves, shield, yellow gown, head covering
  • Lack of protective Equipment; N-95 masks were reused by different healthcare staff, shortage on gowns and cleaning supplies
  • Decline of medical staff mental health
  • Shortage in medical staff due to illness and contracting Covid-19
  • Code Blues and increase in staff loosing patients
